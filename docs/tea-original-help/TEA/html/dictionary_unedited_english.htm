The Unedited English Dictionary includes all the words and phrases available to the author and has approximately 1,050,000 entries. It was compiled from a wide variety of sources and (as the title suggests) has not been rigorously edited – it will inevitably include a number of inaccuracies and inconsistencies.

Unedited English also contains all the available Dictionary Definitions and Citations, including those for words and phrases listed in the smaller Core English and Edited English dictionaries. This avoids duplication and ensures the cross-references between Definitions will always be resolved.

About 195,000 entries include lexicographical information from the dictionary developed for WordNet. Most Dictionary Definitions include a variety of cross-references to related entries; see Navigating between Definitions for information about how to use the cross-references.

Around 140,000 entries include Citations supporting the Edited English entries. In addition, the source is known for five other common classes of word and these are given special tags, as follows:

Tag Meaning
(0) UK place name
(1) US place name
(2) Other place name
(3) Entry in Webster's New International Dictionary 2nd Edition
(4) Word in the Complete Works of Shakespeare
(5) Word in the King James Bible

The majority of entries have no Definition, but web searches using the Edit|External Lookup Commands will usually reveal their meaning.
