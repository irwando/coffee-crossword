Punctuation Matching

If you include punctuation as part of a Template, TEA will only show words that have that punctuation at the specified position (and no more). Space, hyphen and apostrophe are examples of punctuation characters that are commonly searched for.

If you include a specific punctuation character, TEA expects to match that character exactly. For example, if you say a hyphen must occur after the third letter, then words that have hyphens after the third letter and sixth letter won't be matched. (If Ignore unused punctuation is not checked in the Options Pattern Tab, then TEA expects to match all punctuation characters exactly, even those never included in the pattern. In this situation, if you say a hyphen must occur after the third letter, then words that have a hyphen after the third letter and a space after the sixth letter won't be matched.)

If you want to search for one of the Metacharacters that have a specific meaning in pattern matching, you must precede it by the Escape Character (\) to indicate that you want the literal character to be matched.

Case differences and accents are normally ignored, but you can search for a letter in a specific case or with a specific accent by preceding it with the Escape Character (\).

Examples

....-..-.. finds eleven letter words hyphenated with a 4-2-2 pattern 
pick-me-up
well-to-do
* ..d finds phrases where the last word is a three-letter word ending with D 
dead end
rear end
seed pod
launch pad
.......\! finds 5-letter answers ending with an exclamation mark 
my hat!
oh boy!
Whaam!
...-....... ... with Ignore unused punctuation checked, finds 
Gay-Lussac's law
pre-emptive bid
...-....... ... with Ignore unused punctuation unchecked, finds just 
pre-emptive bid
\A.... finds 5-letter words starting with a capital A 
Ascot
*'*'*'* finds words containing at least three apostrophes 
fo'c's'le
